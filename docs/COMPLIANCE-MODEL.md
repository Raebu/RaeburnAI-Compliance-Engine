# Compliance model

This project treats compliance as a structured, evidence-backed operating system rather than a static checklist.

## Assessment lifecycle

1. Register the AI system.
2. Classify context and risk.
3. Select applicable frameworks.
4. Run policy-as-code checks.
5. Attach evidence.
6. Assign residual risks and remediation actions.
7. Review and approve.
8. Monitor changes and reassess.

## Framework coverage

### EU AI Act

- Risk classification
- High-risk AI governance
- Transparency obligations
- Human oversight
- Technical documentation
- Post-market monitoring

### GDPR

- Lawful basis
- DPIA
- Data minimisation
- Retention
- Processor and supplier governance
- DSAR readiness

### ISO/IEC 42001

- AI policy
- Governance roles
- Lifecycle controls
- AI risk management
- Monitoring and continual improvement

### ISO/IEC 27001

- Access control
- Asset inventory
- Logging and monitoring
- Supplier security
- Incident management
- Business continuity

### UK AI guidance

- Safety
- Transparency
- Fairness
- Accountability
- Contestability and redress

## Output

Each assessment produces:

- Compliance score
- Risk classification
- Passed and failed controls
- Evidence gaps
- Remediation roadmap
- Control references
- Timestamped audit output

## Important disclaimer

Outputs are compliance decision-support and should be validated by qualified legal, security and compliance professionals before being relied upon for formal certification, audit or regulatory filings.
